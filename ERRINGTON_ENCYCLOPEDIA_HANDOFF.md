## Shared Copilot Plan Summary

The shared Copilot conversation established the following project direction.

### Core Operating Mode

The next assistant should work in a high-verification mode:

1. Do not guess.
2. Separate verified facts from inference and uncertainty.
3. Prefer conservative horticultural guidance when sources disagree.
4. Do not invent taxonomy, timing windows, pest lists, or climate figures.
5. Treat Errington-local calendar data as the primary timing authority.

### Intended Encyclopedia Scope

The encyclopedia is meant to be:

1. Fully localized to Errington, BC.
2. Focused on both edible crops and ornamentals.
3. Structured as a multi-chapter, print-ready reference.
4. Built with per-plant entries, not just category overviews.
5. Supported by per-plant illustration briefs and per-plant AI image prompts.

### Planned Table Of Contents

The shared plan converged on a structure like this:

1. Front matter and usage notes.
2. Errington climate, frost dates, microclimates, and calendar interpretation.
3. Botany foundations for gardeners.
4. Soil, water, fertility, and coastal growing conditions.
5. Vegetable crops.
6. Perennial edibles, fruits, and berries.
7. Herbs and medicinal plants.
8. Annual flowers and bedding plants.
9. Perennial ornamentals.
10. Bulbs, corms, tubers, and rhizomes.
11. Shrubs, vines, and small ornamental trees.
12. BC natives and ecological gardening.
13. Pests, diseases, and integrated management.
14. Garden design, succession sowing, and season extension.
15. Appendices including spacing tables, bloom tables, glossary, and index.

### Per-Plant Entry Requirements

Each plant entry is expected to include, where verifiable:

1. Common name.
2. Latin name.
3. Botanical family.
4. Life cycle.
5. Growth habit.
6. Root type or storage organ.
7. Leaf arrangement and shape.
8. Flower structure.
9. Fruit or seed structure where relevant.
10. Errington-specific sowing, transplanting, bloom, harvest, and frost notes.
11. Soil, water, fertility, spacing, light, pruning, staking, and maintenance notes.
12. Relevant pests and diseases with conservative local guidance.

### Illustration System Requirements

The agreed illustration system is per-plant, not just per chapter or per group.

Each plant should get:

1. A botanical illustration brief.
2. A matching AI illustration prompt.

Each illustration brief should specify:

1. Whole plant habit.
2. Root system.
3. Leaf arrangement.
4. Leaf shape.
5. Flower structure.
6. Fruit or seed pod if relevant.
7. Storage organ if relevant.
8. Required labeled callouts.

Illustration style requirements:

1. Hand-drawn botanical plate style.
2. Clean linework.
3. Soft neutral palette.
4. Consistent labeling conventions.
5. Clear structure-focused composition rather than decorative styling.

Illustration prompt template baseline:

"Hand-drawn botanical plate of [plant], clean linework, whole plant habit, root system, leaf arrangement, flower or fruit close-up, labeled structures, modern botanical illustration style."
